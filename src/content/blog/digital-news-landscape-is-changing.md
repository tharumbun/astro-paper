Max Tani at Semafor wrote this magnetic headline that got my attention: "[It’s back to the future for a diminished digital news business](https://www.semafor.com/article/04/23/2023/its-back-to-the-future-for-a-diminished-digital-news-business)".

The digital news business is in a state of flux. In the past, news organizations relied on social media platforms to drive traffic to their websites. However, these platforms have become increasingly hostile to news organizations, and they are now competing with news organizations for advertising revenue. As a result, many news organizations are struggling to make money.

Also good read: [The end of the BuzzFeed era in news](https://www.semafor.com/article/04/20/2023/the-end-of-the-buzzfeed-era-in-news)

In response to this challenge, some news organizations are returning to the past. They are focusing on homepage traffic, blogging, and niche email newsletters. These are all foundational concepts of digital media in the early 2000s.

It is too early to say whether this strategy will be successful. However, it is clear that the digital news business is in need of a new model. The old model is no longer sustainable.
